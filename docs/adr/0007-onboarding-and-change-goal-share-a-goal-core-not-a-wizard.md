# Onboarding and change-goal share a goal-core, not a wizard

Two route-level flows create a race-anchored **Goal**: first-run **onboarding** (`(onboarding)/index.tsx`) and the **change-goal wizard** (`(app)/goal/new.tsx`). They look near-identical — both step through `StepRaceDetails → StepRaceGoal`, both call `createMyRaceWithGoal`, and today they duplicate the race-target orchestration (`buildRaceTarget`, `targetSeconds`, `todayIso`, the race-date guard) verbatim. The obvious "fix" is to merge them into one parameterised wizard. We deliberately **do not**. The two flows share a **goal-core spine** (the `StepRaceDetails → StepRaceGoal` pair plus the race-target payload builder, extracted to live once) but stay **two thin orchestrators**, because their surrounding context, their VDOT semantics, and their terminal effects genuinely differ — unifying them would replace honest duplication with a thicket of `if (mode === "onboarding")` branches.

The decisive asymmetry is VDOT. Per ADR-0006 a past race result is **mandatory at onboarding** (it establishes the athlete-level VDOT before plan generation), and the change-goal wizard **reuses** that persisted VDOT rather than re-asking — so onboarding *captures* fitness while the wizard *assumes* it. With fitness goals gone (ADR-0005), `StepChooseType` and the race/fitness branch are deleted and every Goal is race-anchored, which is what lets the shared spine be a single straight line rather than a fork.

## Consequences

- **Two orchestrators stay; the shared surface is a spine, not a flow.** Each route owns its own ordering and its own terminal effect — onboarding sets `hasCompletedOnboarding = true`, the wizard `router.replace`s to the tabs. What they share is the goal-core *components* (`StepRaceDetails`, `StepRaceGoal`) and the *pure helpers* that build the `createMyRaceWithGoal` payload, hoisted into one module so the race-target logic can't drift between the two copies that exist today.

- **The schedule/VDOT split is intentional and is the surprising part.** The wizard *reuses* VDOT (athlete-stable, established once at onboarding) but *re-confirms* the Schedule step (seeded from the athlete). A reader who sees the wizard apply "reuse the athlete-level fact" to VDOT but not to `availableDays`/`sessionsPerWeek` will read it as an inconsistency. It is deliberate: weekly availability genuinely shifts at training-block boundaries (a new goal is a new block), so a seeded confirm-in-context earns its place; a past race time does not change, so re-entering it is pure friction. Availability also has a permanent home in `account/edit` — the wizard's confirm is a convenience, not the only editor.

- **`StepPlan` is deleted, not shared.** The wizard's start-date step collected a value the client never sent and the backend never read — `createMyRaceWithGoal` hardcodes `startDate` to today (`races.ts`) and its args don't accept one. Removing it makes the wizard honest and aligns both flows on "the plan starts today." Deferred plan-start, if ever wanted, returns as a real backend feature, not vestigial UI.

- **Onboarding-only vs wizard-only steps are accepted, not abstracted away.** Onboarding prepends identity capture (`StepWelcome`, `StepProfile`, `StepExperience`, `StepSchedule`) and appends the mandatory race-result step; the wizard appends only the seeded Schedule confirm. These are the parts that would have become mode-branches under a unified wizard — keeping them in separate orchestrators is the whole point.

- **Rejected — one unified wizard.** A single component parameterised by `mode` would have to branch on entry context (no Athlete profile yet vs Athlete + VDOT present), on whether the race-result step appears, on schedule ordering, and on the terminal side-effect. That is four mode-forks over a two-step shared spine — more conditional surface than the duplication it removes.

- **Rejected — fully independent flows.** Leaving the race-target helpers copy-pasted in both routes is what we have today; the two copies will drift the first time the target model changes. Sharing the spine and the payload builder is cheap and removes that drift without coupling the flows' structure.
