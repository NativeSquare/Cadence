# Soma Readiness corroborates the post-session triage (it never acts alone)

The post-session capture extracts the **Concern tier** (`none` / `watch` / `act`) from the Runner's voice debrief alone — Soma biometrics are never crossed with what the Runner said. So a Runner who debriefs "felt fine" the morning after an HRV crash gets no acknowledgment, and the qualitative signal floats free of the quantitative one the market leaves joined-but-unread. We want to close that gap **without** reopening the autonomous, biometric-triggered plan changes that ADR-0003 deliberately removed.

We let **Soma Readiness corroborate the triage, strictly on the Decide side, never on the Act side.** Concretely:

- **Where it lands — triage, not plan logic.** A deterministic pre-fetch computes a normalized **Readiness** block for the workout's day — `{ hrvZScore, restingHrDelta, sleepHours, sleepQuality, noSignal }` — from Soma's retained daily summaries and sleep, joined on `journalEntry.dayKey`. The block is injected into the *same* single `deriveSignals` LLM call that already extracts signals; the LLM reads voice **+** Readiness. This does **not** revive ADR-0001's ban: that ban is on LLM-as-judge in *plan logic* (the Act side). Triage is the Decide side — the Runner still decides, the Engine reshape stays the deterministic `buildEasedWorkout`.

- **A pre-fetch, not a harness.** The Runner reached for an agentic tool-calling loop ("build our own harness"). We rejected it: the data needed at Mark Done is small and *known* (today's readiness, recent load), so there is nothing for an agent to *explore*. A harness would add latency, cost, and failure surface to the synchronous all-or-nothing capture (ADR-0004) for no discovery benefit. The agentic reading-tools harness already exists where exploration belongs — the chat Coach (`reading.ts`), the **pull** side. The post-session **push** gets a fixed deterministic pre-fetch feeding the existing judgment.

- **Corroborate, never originate — a monotonic ratchet, guaranteed by code.** The LLM emits two tiers: `concernFromVoice` (its read of the words alone) and `concernFinal` (its read with Readiness). A deterministic clamp then enforces `concernFromVoice ≤ concernFinal ≤ min(act, concernFromVoice + 1)`. So Readiness may raise the tier by **at most one step** (`none → watch`, `watch → act`), may **never skip** (`none → act` is impossible), and may **never downgrade** (a reassuring metric never silences a worried Runner). A `none → act` jump — which would be `hrv_low_v1` reborn — is structurally unreachable, not merely discouraged by the prompt. The judgment stays LLM-owned; the invariant that keeps us consistent with ADR-0003 is owned by the clamp.

- **The Readiness snapshot is frozen onto the entry.** `readiness` (and `concernFromVoice`) are persisted on the `journalEntry`, not recomputed on read. The decision log must be **durable and self-contained**: if the Runner later disconnects Garmin and Soma purges the raw summaries, a compute-on-read would retroactively lose the context that justified a past decision, and Restitution ("same context as May 12 — HRV at −1.8, you pushed through, lost 10 days") would collapse. Persisting trades a little denormalization for a moat that survives provider churn. `concernFromVoice` is kept alongside `concern` because the voice↔body *disagreement* is the cross-signal pattern the wedge exists to learn from.

- **Soma is opportunistic, never a point of failure.** No wearable, <7 samples in 14 days, or a failed Soma read all resolve to `readiness: { noSignal: true }`: the block is not injected, the tier is voice-only (today's behavior), and the capture proceeds. The pre-fetch is a best-effort read *before* the flaky LLM call and any DB write, so a Soma failure is caught and degraded — it can never break a Mark Done. ADR-0004's all-or-nothing guarantee around the LLM + writes is preserved unchanged.

## Consequences

- **This is a deliberate, bounded reversal of one ADR-0003 line — "the Engine acts on no Soma signal" — and only that line.** ADR-0003 removed *autonomous* triggers (the Engine deciding *and* acting). This does neither: Soma informs a *triage* the Runner acts on. The `hrv_low_v1` *trigger* stays dead; what returns is the z-score *computation* (`getHrvReadiness`-style math, resurrected as a shared pure helper) as a triage input. A future reader who sees HRV math return after ADR-0003 should read it as triage corroboration, not a revived trigger.
- **Soma can now influence whether the Runner is interrupted.** Because `watch → act` is reachable via Readiness, more sessions will cross into `act` and run the conflict search, so the ease prompt appears slightly more often. This is intended — it is the whole point of crossing the signals — but it is a real behavioral change, not just narration.
- **The clamp is the safety boundary and is unit-tested as a truth table** (3 voice tiers × {good / poor / noSignal} Readiness → expected stored tier). A prompt-only rule was rejected precisely because this invariant is too load-bearing to leave to prose. LLM violations are clamped silently; a dev-only `console.warn` surfaces them during rollout without standing telemetry.
- **Schema grows by two persisted fields** (`readiness`, `concernFromVoice`), against the "defer the context snapshot until there's a reader" stance noted on `journalEntry.decision`. The reader here is real and near-term (Restitution + Outcome labeling — the moat), and the snapshot is not reconstructable after a provider disconnect, so the durability argument overrides minimum-viable-infra in this one case.
- **Body battery and stress remain excluded** from Readiness: they are real-time and contaminated by the just-finished run, so they carry no pre-session readiness signal. They stay read-only Analytics context.
