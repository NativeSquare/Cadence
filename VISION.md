# Vision

---

## What

Cadence is an AI running coach that crosses your watch's biometric data with how you're actually doing — so when life gets in the way, it coaches around it instead of ignoring it. The longer you run with it, the better it gets at coaching you.

## Why Now

GPS watch penetration among French regular runners has reached 85–93%, meaning the biometric data already exists for every target user — Cadence doesn't need to generate it, only interpret it. At the same time, the dominant structured-plan apps (Runna, acquired by Strava April 2025) have been publicly linked to injury spikes from plans that ignore fatigue and life context, creating visible distrust in algorithmic plans. Race registrations across all distances in France are growing year-on-year, and the majority of new entrants are women — a segment the existing apps systematically underserve: no current tool accounts for hormonal cycles, a factor that shapes training readiness for roughly half the target population. The conditions are: abundant raw data, broken trust in the current category leader, and a growing, diverse user base with life complexity that no existing plan handles.

## For Whom

A French runner aged 25–45, training consistently — at least 3 sessions a week, with a race or timed goal on the calendar, any distance. Motivated by personal accomplishment and staying healthy, not by becoming elite. They already own a GPS watch, already use Strava, and have tried or are on a structured plan. Their real fear isn't failing the goal — it's that the plan won't survive contact with their actual life: the work week that wipes out three sessions, the knee that's "never quite healed," the hormonal cycle that makes week three a saboteur, the anxiety of "is this too much?" They currently manage the gap between plan and reality with calendar gymnastics, self-googling, and gut feel — a workaround that proves the pain is real.

## Market

~146,000 marathon finishers per year in France; ~12.4M runners, ~8M regular. 85% GPS watch ownership. ~50% already on an app or online coach — Cadence displaces an existing behavior, doesn't create one. The competitive band is €12–20/mo; the dominant players (Runna at €19.99/mo, RunMotion at €14.99/mo) set price expectations. Global AI-native coaching (Humango, athletica.ai, TrainAsONE) shows the category can retain at 13+ months average when adaptation is genuine. The serviceable market is the gap between free/hardware-bundled tools (Garmin Coach, Kiprun) and €150+/mo human coaches — currently owned by no single defensible player in France.

## Business Model

Single Pro subscription at €18.99/mo (30%-off annual), hard paywall, 14-day free trial. Rationale: positioned just below Runna's standalone price, above RunMotion, in the band French runners have already demonstrated willingness to pay. No freemium tier diluting the coaching relationship — the trial is the acquisition surface.

## Go-to-Market

**First 10 customers:** Founder-led outreach at a weekly Saturday running meetup on the Quai de Seine in Paris — ~40 runners per session, recurring, direct access. Show up as a runner, earn trust through the community, convert through conversation.

**Path to 100:** Word of mouth from the first club cohort (runners with a visible story — "it caught my overtraining before the 20K week"), combined with short-form content on TikTok and Instagram showing real plan-change events in action. Content is seeded by real user stories from the club, not produced in the abstract.

## Moat

The outcome-labeled decision log: Cadence records not just what the runner decided (rest, adapt, push) but what happened afterward (the niggle held, the 10-day injury hit, the workout executed cleanly). After 6 months, this is a personalized dataset of _decision × context → outcome_ that no competitor can copy — they'd need to start over with that runner. The value of Cadence compounds with time; the switching cost grows with every labeled decision. This is not the log itself that's the moat — it's the log labeled by its result, accumulated across months, for that specific athlete.

## Timeline

- **3 months**: 50 paying users, €800+ MRR — 70%+ of free-trial users converted, 80%+ still active after month 1. App live on the App Store. At least one real Engine trigger (illness, missed week, life event) per active user in the wild.
- **6 months**: 200 paying users, €3,500+ MRR — 80%+ still active at month 3, 30%+ of new sign-ups from referral (WOM flywheel is real), at least one piece of content driving 20+ sign-ups directly (TikTok/Instagram channel validated).
- **18 months**: 1,500 paying users, €25K+ MRR — recognized as the go-to AI running coach in France, a second distribution channel working beyond WOM and content (race partnerships, coach referrals, or Strava integration), users at 12+ months showing measurably lower override rates than newer users (compounding personalization is provably real).
- **3 years**: 10,000+ paying users, €180K+ MRR — France owned, first expansion market live (French-speaking: Belgium, Switzerland, or Quebec), churn measurably lower for users with 18+ months of decision history, Cadence is the community-cited reference answer when French runners ask "which app actually adapts to my life?"
